Why Turkey Is The Most Important Country In The World Right Now

May 25, 2025  ·  Explaining The World

There is a country that is simultaneously a member of NATO and a buyer of Russian weapons. A country that controls the only maritime passage between the Black Sea and the Mediterranean. A country whose leader has sat across the table from Putin, Biden, Zelensky, and the leaders of the Arab world — and extracted concessions from all of them. That country is Turkey. And if you are not paying attention to it, you are not paying attention to geopolitics.

The Geography That Makes Turkey Irreplaceable

The Bosphorus Strait is 31 kilometres long and less than 700 metres wide at its narrowest point. Every ship moving between Russia's Black Sea ports and the rest of the world must pass through it. Turkey controls this strait under the 1936 Montreux Convention — a legal framework that gives Ankara the power to close the passage to warships during times of conflict. When Russia invaded Ukraine in 2022, Turkey invoked this convention and closed the Bosphorus to all warships. In one decision, Ankara trapped the Russian Black Sea Fleet. This is not a small power. This is a country that can strangle a nuclear superpower's naval access with a single legal instrument.

The NATO Member That Bought Russian Weapons

In 2019 Turkey purchased the S-400 missile defence system from Russia. This was not an accident or a miscalculation. It was a deliberate strategic signal. NATO operates on the principle of interoperability — all members use compatible weapons systems so they can fight together. By buying Russian weapons Turkey told Washington directly: we are in this alliance on our terms, not yours. The United States responded by removing Turkey from the F-35 fighter jet programme. Turkey responded by not caring. Erdogan had made his point. Turkey cannot be taken for granted.

Every major power needs Turkey more than Turkey needs them. That asymmetry is the source of Erdogan's leverage.

The Mediator Nobody Expected

When Russia and Ukraine needed a channel for grain export negotiations in 2022, they did not call Washington or Brussels. They called Ankara. Turkey hosted the negotiations that produced the Black Sea Grain Initiative — a deal that kept global food prices from catastrophic collapse. Turkey also facilitated the largest prisoner exchange of the war. A country that is supposed to be a NATO ally was simultaneously maintaining functional relationships with Moscow, Kyiv, Washington, and Brussels. This is not confusion. This is masterful strategic positioning.

Erdogan And The Art Of Strategic Ambiguity

Recep Tayyip Erdogan has been in power since 2003. In that time he has transformed Turkey from a country trying to join the European Union into a regional power with independent foreign policy ambitions. He has blocked NATO membership for Sweden and Finland for months — extracting security guarantees and extradition agreements before finally agreeing. He has maintained trade with Russia while officially condemning the invasion. He has sold armed drones to Ukraine while refusing to join Western sanctions on Moscow. Every major power needs Turkey more than Turkey needs them. That asymmetry is the source of Erdogan's leverage.

Why This Matters For The Next Decade

Turkey is not a swing state. It is a pivot state. The difference matters. A swing state can go one way or another. A pivot state determines which way the entire balance tips. As NATO faces its most serious internal tensions since the Cold War, as Russia recalibrates after Ukraine, as the Middle East enters a period of profound realignment — Turkey sits at every intersection. The country that controls the Bosphorus, bridges Europe and Asia, maintains relationships with every side of every conflict, and possesses the second largest army in NATO is not a regional power. It is a global one. The world is beginning to understand this. The question is whether it is too late.

Turkey will not be ignored for much longer. The next decade of geopolitics will be shaped significantly by decisions made in Ankara. Understanding Turkey is not optional for anyone who wants to understand the world.
