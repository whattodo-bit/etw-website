Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we receive most often about the publication, our process, and working with us.

About ETW

ETW is an independent geopolitical publication that produces original long-form analysis every Sunday. We focus on the structural forces — geography, economics, demography — that shape global affairs over the long term.

All articles are produced by the ETW Editorial desk. We publish under a single editorial voice rather than individual bylines to keep the analysis focused on the argument, not the author.

No. ETW is fully independent. We accept no political funding, no sponsor influence, and no advertising that shapes editorial direction.

Articles

One article every Sunday. We prioritise depth and accuracy over volume.

Most pieces run between 2,000 and 4,000 words — roughly an 8 to 15 minute read. Length is dictated by the subject, never padded.

No. Our work is deliberately slow. We write about forces and trajectories, not the story of the week.

Please contact us before republishing. Short excerpts with attribution and a link back to the original are generally fine.

Collaboration

Occasionally, for specialist subjects. See our Collaborate page for the current criteria.

Yes, on a selective basis. Please email contact@explainingtheworld.com with the outlet, topic, and timeline.

We take on a limited number of private research briefs each quarter. Details are on the Collaborate page.

Legal

No. Everything we publish is independent analysis and personal opinion. It is not professional advice of any kind and should not be relied on as such.

We correct factual errors promptly and transparently. Please email contact@explainingtheworld.com with the specific article, paragraph, and source of the correction.
