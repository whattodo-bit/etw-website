Privacy Policy

Explaining The World (“ETW”) respects your privacy. This policy explains what little information we collect, how it is used, and what is never done with it.

What We Collect

We do not collect personal data from visitors to this website. We do not run tracking cookies, analytics identifiers, advertising pixels, or fingerprinting of any kind.

Email Contact

If you email us at contact@explainingtheworld.com, we store your message only for as long as is necessary to reply. Email addresses are not added to any mailing list, shared with anyone, or used for marketing.

Third-Party Fonts

This website loads typefaces from Google Fonts. When your browser requests those files, Google may log your IP address and basic request headers as part of its standard infrastructure. ETW does not receive or control this data. If you wish to avoid it, blocking Google Fonts in your browser will not break the site — it will simply use fallback typefaces.

ETW Assistant

If the website offers an AI assistant, conversations may be used to improve the content and services offered by ETW. No personal data is stored beyond the current session. Please do not enter personal, financial, or sensitive information into the assistant.

What We Never Do

We do not sell, rent, trade, or share your data with advertisers or third parties. There is no ETW data product. There never will be.
